People


The people of Rovinj are incredibly friendly and hospitable. They love to share their culture, and are happy to offer good restaurants, hospitality, and sights to see to foreigners. Another thing to note is that the people of Rovinj are widely versed in English, like much of Croatia, making it a great spot for visitors!

Fishing is incredibly important to the community, as it is a fishing town and contributes immensely to their economy, so you will often see fishermen out on the docks with their boats. You can also take a gander at their bountiful fish markets in the morning. As fishing and the sea is so important to the people here, it strongly effects their cuisine and their traditions.

You can also stroll through the charming, crooked streets and chat with the artisans that sell their product throughout the town. Many people use art as means to live in Rovinj and sell some truly stunning pieces.

With Rovinj's culture being tied very closely with the sea, lots of their customs relate to it, such as the Batana. The Batana is a wooden boat that you can go and see being built in the town center every summer. Later in the summer, after the boat is constructed, you can go and see its first sailing. After that, visitors and citizens alike can take a ride in the Batana as a part of the “Batana with Fishing Light Parade and a Dinner at the Spacio” event. This is a major event for the people of Rovinj.

The streets of Rovinj

One thing that shows how much the people of Rovinj care about their town is the streets. The streets of Rovinj are sprinkled with decorative flowers, art, shops and bakeries, and architecture that has character.
