Rovinj is a quaint fishing community on the Istrian peninsula, that differs in many ways to other Croatian cities but is a must see. It is a formidable spot to get away from the bustling tourism on the Dalmatian coast as although it still gets visitors, its typically missed by many tourists.

Due to its geographical closeness to Italy, you can tell by the architecture and atmosphere of the city that it has been affected by Italian culture. If you're looking for a charming and rustic atmosphere with fresh seafood and wine that is right off this coast, then say no more.

Although there are historical landmarks, it is not what they are known for in terms of tourism compared to other Croatian cities like Korcula or Dubrovnik, however, it has its own unique specialties. Walk the beautiful, narrow cobble stone streets, and stop in at the many boutiques that line the streets. Maybe get some gelato and sit by the beautiful harbor. Rovinj is a nice spot for a relaxing, more slow-paced vacation.
